### Fabrication Steps

The process can be divided into two main sections: forming the source/drain electrodes (Lift-off process) and patterning the channel and encapsulation layers.

#### 1. Preparing the Substrate

* **i.** A **Mylar** layer is adhered to a **silicon substrate** by using a thin layer of **wax**.

#### 2. Lift-off Process to Pattern Au Source and Drain

* **ii.** **Spin coat** a **photoresist (SU-8)** layer and conduct **photolithography** to pattern the SU-8 layer. (See image ii.)
* **iii.** **Sputter** a thin layer of **Au** onto the surface. (See image iii.)
* **iv.** Immerse the sample in a solution to **remove the SU-8 layer** and the Au layer on top, leaving the **source and drain Au patterns** on the Mylar layer. (See image iv.)

#### 3. Channel and Encapsulation Layer Patterning

* **v.** **Spin coat** a **PEDOT:PSS layer** on the surface (the active p-type channel material). (See image v.)
* **vi.** **Spin coat** a **photoresist (S1800)** layer and conduct **photolithography** to pattern the S1800 layer. (See image vi.)
* **vii.** Use **RIE (Reactive Ion Etching)** to remove exposed **PEDOT:PSS layer** and remove the S1800 layer afterwards. (This patterns the channel.) (See image vii.)
* **viii.** **Spin coat** an **SU-8 layer** and conduct **photolithography** to pattern make an opening the channel. **Separate the Mylar layer**. (This is the final flexible OECT device, cross-section A-A' shown with the dashed line.) (See image viii.)
