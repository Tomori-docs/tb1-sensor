* Optical sensors are based on measuring either the **intensity change** in one or more light beams (**intensity sensors**) or **phase changes** in the light beams (**interferometric sensors**) caused by their interaction or interference.
* The techniques used in the case of intensity sensors include **light scattering** (both Rayleigh and Raman), **spectral transmission changes** (i.e., simple attenuation of transmitted light due to absorption), **microbending** or **radiative losses**, **reflectance changes** and **changes in the modal properties of the fiber**.
* The simplest optical intensity sensor is a **photodetector**. In a **reverse-biased P-N junction detector**, electron-hole pairs generated by the incident optical energy increases the carrier density. **PIN** and **avalanche-type photodiodes** are also used in several applications.
