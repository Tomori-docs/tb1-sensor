
## üìÑ Summary of Sensor Types

| Sensor Type | Key Effect | Example of Sensing Material |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Conductometric** | Change in electrical conductivity | Metals |
| **Capacitive** | Change in capacitance | Si |
| **Piezoelectric** | Generation of electrical charge from mechanical stress | PZT, lead metaniobate, lead titanate, PVDF |
| **Magnetostrictive** | Change in dimensions due to magnetic field | Ferrites, and metallic alloys |
| **Piezoresistive** | Change in electrical resistance from mechanical stress | Metals, Si, polymeric/carbon-based composites, ceramics and oxides |
| **Optical** | Changes in light properties (e.g., intensity, reflection) | Semiconductors |
| **Resonant** | Change in resonant frequency due to physical factors | Si |
| **Transistor-based** | Change in transistor properties (e.g., current, voltage) | Si |
| **Acoustic** | Changes in sound waves or vibrations | PZT, PVDF |
| **Temperature** | Change in electrical properties due to temperature | RTDs, thermistors, thermocouples, IR sensors, thermopiles |
| **Polymer** | Change in physical/electrical properties due to stimuli | PVDF, PDMS-silver nanoparticle composite |

---

### üìù Key Notes

* Key parameters in sensors' performance include **sensitivity, selectivity, stability, accuracy, resolution, repeatability**, etc.
* Two basic types of noise: **Inherent** and **interference** noise.
